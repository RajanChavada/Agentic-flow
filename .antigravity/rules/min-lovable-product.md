# Minimum Lovable Product (MLP)

> "If it's not lovable, it's not viable in the market anymore." -- Elena Verna

We build **Minimum Lovable Products**, not MVPs. MVPs ship skeletons people tolerate. MLPs ship the earliest version that makes people say *"This is actually nice."*

## Hierarchy of SaaS Needs

| Level | Question |
|-------|----------|
| **Functional** | Does it work? Load, not crash, do what it promises? |
| **Reliable** | Is it secure and dependable? |
| **Usable** | Is it easy and intuitive? Can I figure it out? |
| **Lovable** | Does it delight me? Does interacting leave me with a positive feeling? |

**Our bar:** Don't stop at functional or usable. Aim for lovable.

## What Lovable Looks Like

- **Fast, obvious, opinionated** -- a "wait, this is actually nice" moment
- **Love marks** -- unexpected, delightful moments of humanity (easter eggs, personality, celebration of wins)
- **Human connection** -- how the product talks, handles errors, celebrates success

## Before Shipping: Ask

> "Is this lovable?" -- Not "does it work" or "is it ready."

## Practical Guidelines

1. **Start with the customer, not the feature list** -- find what would make them fall in love
2. **Build lovemarks into the roadmap** -- delight moments, personality in small interactions
3. **Use AI to get there faster** -- "How can I make this interaction more lovable?"
4. **Keep it minimal** -- MLP != gold-plating. What's the simplest thing that creates a genuine emotional moment?

## When Planning Features

Use `search_web` to research MLP best practices. Look for:
- Emotional design patterns
- Micro-interaction libraries
- Delight moment case studies
- Competitor UX teardowns
